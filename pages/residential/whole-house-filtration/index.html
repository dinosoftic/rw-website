Why Whole House Filtration?

While point-of-use filters only treat water at one faucet, whole house filtration treats ALL the water entering your home. This means clean water for:

  • Drinking and cooking
  • Showering and bathing
  • Laundry
  • Dishwashing
  • All household uses

This comprehensive approach protects your entire plumbing system and all water-using appliances.

What Whole House Filtration Removes

  • Sediment and particulates
  • Chlorine and chloramines
  • Bad tastes and odors
  • Organic compounds
  • Some pesticides and herbicides

For homes with specific issues like iron, hardness, or high TDS, we can design a multi-stage system that addresses all your water quality concerns.

Whole House vs. Point-of-Use: Why Treating All Your Water Matters

Contaminants in your water don't just affect what you drink—they affect everything. Chlorine dries out your skin and hair in the shower. Sediment clogs your washing machine and dishwasher valves. Iron stains your toilets, sinks, and laundry. Hard water builds up inside your water heater, reducing efficiency and shortening its life.

For drinking water specifically, a point-of-use reverse osmosis (RO) system is the gold standard—it removes up to 99% of dissolved contaminants and delivers the purest water possible right from your kitchen faucet. We do not recommend pitcher filters, refrigerator filters, or basic faucet-mount filters as a long-term solution. These consumer-grade filters have limited capacity, inconsistent performance, and do not remove the range of contaminants that a professional RO system does.

A whole-house filtration system treats water at the point of entry, so every tap, appliance, and fixture in your home gets clean, filtered water. Paired with a point-of-use RO system for drinking water, this combination gives you the most comprehensive water quality solution available.

Our technicians will perform a free in-home water test, identify exactly what's in your water, and recommend the right combination of filtration to solve it. No guesswork, no one-size-fits-all approach—just a system built for your water.
